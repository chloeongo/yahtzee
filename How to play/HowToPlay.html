How to play

Objective

Roll dice for scoring combinations, and get the highest total score.

Game Rules

Each player gets a scorecard with different categories to fill in. Players take turns rolling the dice.
After each roll, they can choose to keep some dice and re-roll the rest up to three times. They can choose which
dice to keep and which to re-roll to try and achieve the best combination. After the third roll,
the player must choose a category to score their points in.
Each category on the scorecard can only be used once.

Categories

Ones, Twos, Threes, Fours, Fives, Sixes: The player's score is the sum of the dice showing the corresponding number.
Three of a Kind: Three dice showing the same number, score is the sum of all dice.
Four of a Kind: Four dice showing the same number, score is the sum of all dice.
Full House: Three dice of one number and two of another, score is 25 points.
Small Straight: Four sequential dice (e.g., 1-2-3-4), score is 30 points.
Large Straight: Five sequential dice (e.g., 1-2-3-4-5), score is 40 points.
Yahtzee: All five dice showing the same number, score is 50 points.
Chance: Score is the sum of all dice, no specific combination required.

Scoring

After choosing a category, the player writes their score for that category on the scorecard.
If the combination achieved does not fit into any category,
the player must choose an empty category to score zero or use the Chance category.

Bonus

Players get a bonus of 35 points if the total of their Ones through Sixes categories is 42 or higher.

End of game

The game ends when all players have filled in their scorecards.
The player with the highest total score wins.
